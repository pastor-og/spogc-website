The heavens declare the glory of God. But when was the last time you actually stopped to listen?

Full sermon · "Wonder That Leads to Worship"
Scripture text will be populated from the Bible API or manual entry. Psalm 19:1–6; John 1:14
1
Creation speaks first

David says the heavens declare — present tense, every day, without pause. God's first language isn't Hebrew or Greek. It's sunrise, tide, birdsong, and the sheer weight of a starry sky.

2
The Word became flesh

John 1:14 says the Word became flesh and dwelt among us. God didn't stay abstract — he moved into the neighborhood. The God who made the oceans stood ankle-deep in one.

3
Wonder becomes stewardship

When you're awed by something, you take care of it. Worship leads to stewardship — of creation, of beauty, of the places and people God has entrusted to us.

3

Go deeper

  • When was the last time something in creation stopped you in your tracks? What was it?
  • Do you tend to rush past beauty, or do you let it slow you down? What would change if you let it?
  • How does the idea that 'the Word became flesh' — that God entered the physical world — change how you think about your own body, your neighborhood, the ocean?
  • What's one thing in God's creation that you could steward better this week?

For a small group or over coffee:

  • What's the most awe-inspiring thing you've ever seen in nature?
  • Do you find it easier to connect with God outdoors or indoors? Why?
  • If creation 'declares the glory of God,' what is it saying right now — this morning, in this place?

The Five-Minute Gaze

Once this week, find something in creation — the ocean, a tree, the sky at dusk — and give it five full minutes of unhurried attention. No phone. No agenda. Just look. Let the beauty speak first. Then ask God: what are you saying through this? Write down one word or phrase that comes to mind. Carry it through the rest of the day.
