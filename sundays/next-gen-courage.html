Next-Gen Courage

1 Timothy 4:12; Joshua 1:9

You don't have to be old enough, experienced enough, or confident enough. You just have to be willing.

The message

▶
Full sermon · "Next-Gen Courage"
Scripture text will be populated from the Bible API or manual entry. 1 Timothy 4:12; Joshua 1:9
1
Youth is not disqualification

Paul tells Timothy not to let anyone dismiss him for his age. God has always called the young, the unprepared, the unlikely. Age is never the issue — availability is.

2
Courage is commanded, not optional

God tells Joshua 'be strong and courageous' three times. It's not a suggestion or a personality trait — it's marching orders, backed by a promise: 'I will be with you.'

3
Stewarding the next generation

Investing in young leaders isn't charity — it's stewardship of the church's future. The question for every generation is: are you making room, or are you holding the door shut?

3

Go deeper

  • When have you felt 'too young' or 'not ready' for something God seemed to be calling you to?
  • Who invested in you when you were younger? What did that look like?
  • Joshua was told to be courageous because God would be with him. Where do you need to hear that promise right now?
  • What's one area of your faith where you've been waiting to feel ready instead of just starting?

For a small group or over coffee:

  • Who's a young person in your life who inspires you? What do you see in them?
  • What's the hardest part about stepping into something before you feel qualified?
  • How can the church do a better job of empowering the next generation without patronizing them?

The Courage Conversation

This week, have one real conversation with someone younger than you about faith — not a lecture, a conversation. Ask what they're thinking about God, what questions they're sitting with, what they wish the church understood. Listen more than you talk. If you are the younger one, initiate a conversation with someone older. Ask them about a time they were afraid and moved forward anyway.
