Full sermon · "The Stewardship of Encouragement"
Scripture text will be populated from the Bible API or manual entry. 1 Thessalonians 5:11; Hebrews 10:24–25
1
Words carry weight

Paul tells the Thessalonians to encourage one another and build each other up. He's not suggesting — he's commanding. Our words aren't neutral; they're either constructing or demolishing.

2
Encouragement is intentional

The writer of Hebrews says to 'consider how to spur one another on.' Consider — it's a deliberate act, not a feeling. Real encouragement costs attention.

3
Stewardship of influence

Every interaction is an opportunity to invest in someone. The question isn't whether you have influence — it's what you're doing with it.

3

Go deeper

  • When I'm stressed, do my words tend to build or bruise? What's underneath that?
  • Who is one person God might be placing on my heart to strengthen this week?
  • What would it look like to encourage someone without trying to fix them?
  • Think of a time when someone's words changed the trajectory of your day — or your life. What made those words land?

For a small group or over coffee:

  • What's something someone said to you that you still carry (in a good way)?
  • Where do you most need encouragement right now — health, family, faith, finances, work?
  • What makes encouragement feel genuine to you (and not cheesy)?

Three Voices

Pick three people this week (different generations if possible). Send each a brief, specific note: what you appreciate about them, what you see God doing in their life, and one simple blessing or prayer. A text works. A handwritten note works better. Don't overthink it — just say what's true.
