Hospitality on Purpose

July 26, 2026 Beach Church · 9 AM OGCMA Speaker
Luke 14:12–24; Romans 15:7

Hospitality isn't about having the right house or the right dishes. It's about having room at the table for someone who wasn't expecting an invitation.

1
Invite the uninvited

Jesus tells the host: stop inviting people who can repay you. Invite the poor, the disabled, the overlooked. Real hospitality is measured by who you'd never think to include.

2
Excuses reveal priorities

In the parable, every invited guest has a reason not to come — land, oxen, a wedding. None are bad reasons. They're just more important to them than the feast. What are your excuses?

3
Acceptance as hospitality

Paul says accept one another as Christ accepted you. That's the benchmark — not tolerance, not politeness, but the radical welcome Jesus extends to everyone who shows up.

3

Go deeper

  • When was the last time you invited someone into your life who couldn't 'return the favor'?
  • Jesus's parable is about people too busy for the feast. What's keeping you too busy for the things that matter most?
  • Who in your neighborhood, workplace, or family circle might be waiting for an invitation they'll never generate on their own?
  • What does it look like to practice hospitality in a digital age — not just in your home?

For a small group or over coffee:

  • What's the most welcoming space you've ever walked into? What made it feel that way?
  • Is there a difference between being friendly and being hospitable?
  • Who is 'the poor, the crippled, the lame, the blind' in your context — who are the people the church tends to overlook?

The Open Table

This week, share a meal with someone you wouldn't normally eat with — a neighbor you don't know, a coworker you've never had coffee with, someone from a different generation or background. It doesn't have to be fancy. Pizza counts. The point is proximity and presence. Before the meal, pray: 'God, help me see this person the way you see them.'
