Rest as Resistance

July 5, 2026 Beach Church · 9 AM Megan Faulkner
Matthew 11:28–30; Genesis 2:2–3

In a culture that rewards exhaustion, choosing rest is one of the most countercultural — and faithful — things you can do.

The message

Full sermon · "Rest as Resistance"
Scripture text will be populated from the Bible API or manual entry. Matthew 11:28–30; Genesis 2:2–3
1
Rest is built into creation

God rested on the seventh day — not from fatigue, but as a statement: it is complete. Sabbath isn't a concession to human weakness; it's embedded in the architecture of the world.

2
Jesus invites the weary

Come to me, all you who are weary. The invitation is to come as you are — tired, burdened, running on empty — and find that Jesus's yoke is actually lighter than the one you've been carrying alone.

3
Rest as trust

Choosing to stop is choosing to believe God is still at work when you aren't. In a productivity-obsessed culture, rest is a radical act of faith.

3

Go deeper

  • What does real rest look like for you — and when was the last time you experienced it?
  • Do you feel guilty when you rest? Where does that guilt come from?
  • Jesus says his yoke is 'easy' and his burden is 'light.' Does your experience of faith feel that way? If not, what's adding weight?
  • What would it take to protect one hour this week as genuinely sacred rest — not productive, not scrolling, just being?

For a small group or over coffee:

  • Do you think rest is something you're good at? Why or why not?
  • What's the difference between rest and avoidance?
  • If God modeled rest in creation, what does that say about how we're supposed to live?

The Sabbath Hour

Choose one hour this week that belongs to rest alone. No phone, no tasks, no productivity. Walk, sit, read, pray, nap — whatever actually replenishes you. Before you begin, read Matthew 11:28–30 slowly. After the hour, notice what changed. This isn't about discipline — it's about trust.
