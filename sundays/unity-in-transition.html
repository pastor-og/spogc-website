Unity in Transition

August 9, 2026 Beach Church · 9 AM OGCMA Speaker
Ephesians 4:1–6

Change is coming — it always is. The question isn't whether you'll face transition, but whether you'll face it together.

The message

Full sermon · "Unity in Transition"
Scripture text will be populated from the Bible API or manual entry. Ephesians 4:1–6
1
Walk worthy of the calling

Paul begins with posture: humility, gentleness, patience, bearing with one another in love. Unity isn't a program — it's a way of being together.

2
Eager to maintain unity

The word is eager — not casual, not passive. Unity takes effort. It doesn't survive on autopilot, especially during seasons of change.

3
One body, many transitions

Churches change. Leadership changes. Communities change. But the 'one Lord, one faith, one baptism' doesn't. Hold the center, release the periphery.

3

Go deeper

  • How do you typically respond to change — do you lean in, resist, or withdraw?
  • Paul lists humility, gentleness, patience, and bearing with one another. Which of these is hardest for you right now?
  • Is there a relationship or community where you've let a disagreement become a division? What would it take to rebuild?
  • What does 'eager to maintain the unity of the Spirit' look like practically in your life this week?

For a small group or over coffee:

  • What's the biggest transition you've navigated as part of a community (church, family, workplace)?
  • Where's the line between healthy disagreement and divisive conflict?
  • How can a church stay unified without pretending everyone agrees on everything?

The Unity Prayer

For three days this week, pray by name for someone you find difficult — in your church, family, or community. Don't pray that they change. Pray that God would give you eyes to see them the way God sees them. On the third day, reach out to them with a small act of kindness or connection. Notice what shifts in you.
